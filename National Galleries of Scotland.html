National Galleries of Scotland

This brief was set to our degree design agency by our mentor from Whitespace, who are a professional design agency based in Edinburgh. The task was to recreate the National Galleries of Scotland web page design, whilst being aware of site specifics; in particular the amount of data the site holds and how it should effectively be displayed.

We brainstormed ideas, learned a lot about web page design and took on our own roles along the way. I illustrated and collated the imagery needed throughout. The aim was to keep the layout modern, but tidy up the pages so it would be more enjoyable to read. It was decided that the overall style could be more modern and colourful which would be more appealing to a wider audience rather than a dated composition. We collaboratively developed the layouts of each page using wireframes, user journeys and digital mock-ups, until the final outcomes were the most efficient in terms of navigation and display. The final design was placed onto InVision which allowed us to show the pages in action.
